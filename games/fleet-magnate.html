What's in the game

🚖

Drive, hire, dispatch

Start by taking fares yourself. Hire drivers when you can afford them. Eventually let managers run the day-to-day while you set strategy.

🚌

Build a bus network

Once your taxi company is on its feet, found Bus Co and assign buses to city routes. Each route earns steady fares per game hour.

📋

Take on contracts

Sign Small, Medium, Large, or Premium contracts. Each ties up vehicles for a set time. Bigger contracts pay more but cost more to fail.

👥

Four manager tiers

Juniors handle ride dispatch. Mids and Seniors scout contracts. Ops managers run bus routes. Each tier comes with its own salary and skills.

⚙️

Upgrades that matter

Boost fares, fuel efficiency, hire caps, scout rates. Install on one vehicle or batch-install across your whole fleet at once.

📊

Detailed reports

See lifetime earnings per vehicle, per manager, per bus route. Find your best earners and your money pits. Pass-only deep analytics.

☁️

Cloud save

Link Google Play Games and your progress syncs across devices. Reinstall, switch phone, keep going — your fleet survives.

🌍

13 languages

Full in-app translation across English, Spanish, Portuguese, German, French, Italian, Russian, Korean, Japanese, Chinese, Indonesian, Hindi, and Turkish.

What makes it different

Most transport-tycoon games either run on autopilot (you just watch numbers go up) or grind you with timers between every action. Fleet Magnate sits in the middle: there are real choices to make — which vehicle to buy, who to hire, which contract to sign, where to send your buses — and the world responds without nagging you.

Every number is honest. Every cost ties back to fuel, salaries, maintenance, and breakdowns that you can actually see in the ledger. Every upgrade does what it says. Every progression milestone unlocks because you earned it, not because a 23-hour timer expired.

The game is free. If you want to support development or skip rewarded video ads, the Drive City Pass and optional Starter Pack are available — both transparent one-time or stable subscription pricing. No surprise renewals, no fake "75% off" pop-ups, no tricks.
